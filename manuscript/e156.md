# DossierGap: a fail-closed AI-assisted pipeline for extracting pivotal-trial data from cardiology regulatory dossiers

---

Do all FDA and EMA pivotal efficacy trials for cardiology new molecular entities reach peer-reviewed publication, or does a dossier-publication gap exist? We assembled 20 cardiology new-molecular-entity approvals 2015–2024 and attempted automated extraction of pivotal-trial hazard ratios, confidence intervals, and randomised N from FDA Medical Reviews and EMA European Public Assessment Reports. The open-source pipeline, DossierGap v0.2.0, uses regex plus document-clustering with fail-closed schema validation and rejects negated counts, chart-axis numbers, and silent-sentinel extractions. Of 15 url-seeded NMEs attempted across 30 source-pairs, two yielded ground-truth-matched extractions (Entresto PARADIGM-HF hazard ratio 0.80 95% CI 0.73 to 0.87; Verquvo VICTORIA 0.90 0.82 to 0.98, both matching NEJM to two decimal places), two noisy extractions were flagged, and 17 failed on structural or semantic grounds. Schema invariants, negation filters, and regression tests prevented silent corruption; hand audit confirmed the two clean rows. Pattern extraction covered approximately 13% of cardiology dossiers and works for Entresto-style template trials but misses secondary-endpoint-adjacent primaries and lipid-only biologics licence applications, implying semantic content scoring is required before Turner-style publication-gap analysis is feasible. Code, audit, and validation data at github.com/mahmood726-cyber/dossiergap.
