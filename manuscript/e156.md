Do all FDA and EMA pivotal efficacy trials for cardiology new molecular entities reach peer-reviewed publication, or does a dossier-publication gap exist? We assembled 20 cardiology new-molecular-entity approvals 2015–2024, with FDA Medical Reviews and EMA European Public Assessment Report PDFs serving as source documents. Open-source pipeline DossierGap extracts trial hazard ratios, confidence intervals, and randomised N from dossier PDFs via regex plus document-clustering. Of 15 url-seeded NMEs attempted across 30 source-pairs, two yielded ground-truth matches: Entresto PARADIGM-HF hazard ratio 0.80 (95% CI 0.73–0.87) and Verquvo VICTORIA 0.90 (0.82–0.98), both matching NEJM exactly. Schema invariants reject negated counts and chart-axis numbers; regression tests prevent silent corruption; both clean rows match NEJM to two decimal places. Pattern extraction succeeds on Entresto-template trials but misses primary results adjacent to subgroup narratives; semantic content scoring is the next required improvement. DossierGap v0.2.0 source code, validation CSV, and per-drug audit are at github.com/mahmood726-cyber/dossiergap; not yet ready for cohort-level Turner inflation analysis.
